I've been struggling to write about these books without making myself cringe. Genuinely my third time writing this post. I can't put my feelings to words in a cool way so I'm just going to write it simply. 

I really, really enjoyed both of [Jordan Mechner](https://www.jordanmechner.com)'s journals.

Mechner created the video games *[Karateka](https://www.jordanmechner.com/en/books/the-making-of-karateka/)* and *[Prince of Persia](https://www.jordanmechner.com/en/books/journals/)*. These books are the journals he kept during the creation of the games.

The books give a fantastic insight into the world of 1980's commercial game development. Seeing Mechner's creative process as he solves design problems across several journal entries is fascinating. 

While the books are about game development, they are still journals at the end of the day. The texts are very personal and delve into his day-to-day life. They're not textbooks or software documentation, but provide enough technical crumbs to keep the nerds satisfied.

My favourite parts were when Mechner was expressing his doubts and worries about the direction of his life. He's very vulnerable and honest with himself throughout.

Start to finish I loved these books and I'd recommend them to anyone. You don't need to be a developer, or even to have played the games (I haven't!) to enjoy reading them. 

Anyone who's creating anything will find inspiration in Mechner's story.

Additional info:
- [Stripe Press](https://press.stripe.com/the-making-of-prince-of-persia) published a deluxe illustrated edition of The Making of Prince of Persia, it's fantastic
- Mechner [published the rest of his journals](https://www.jordanmechner.com/en/library/1993-journals/) for free on his website. What a cool guy!
