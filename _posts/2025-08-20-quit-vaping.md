I used to smoke and now I used to vape. 

They're both boggin, but vaping is less boggin. 

I used disposable vapes then moved to a refillable and kept the habbit for over a year. Eventually I became sick of feeling the *need* to reach for my vape. Always wanting it, always planning when I can next use it, always managing how much juice or puffs I have left. 

The expense is another layer of insanity. 

On the 14th I just decided to stop. I've quit in the past (smoking and vaping) - the biggest factor in whether you're actually successful is being mentally ready to stop. I'm definitely there. 

To motivate myself I threw together this quit and dirty webpage that counts the time since I quit and shows some other tips and motivators. 

I'm hoping the soul crushing shame of having to reset the timer back down to zero is enough incentive to never touch a vape again. 

[Visit the Nicotine Quitter Clock](/work/vape/).

The "Quit Date" on the page is when I quit, but there are options to enter your own details and use this tool for yourself. I had wanted to flesh this out a bit more but to be honest I can't be bothered! 

Enjoy :)
